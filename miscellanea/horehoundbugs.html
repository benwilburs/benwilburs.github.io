A Strange Discovery

One day, while doing a bit of pruning in the backyard, I came across this strikingly coloured bug, living on a bushy plant that I believe is called ‘lion’s tail’ (Leonotis leonurus), which features long rising stems with leaves radiating outwards in tiers, and bright orange flowers which also grow out in circles from whorls on the stem. I had not noticed them before, but there were very many of them, perhaps over one hundred, mostly on the undersides of leaves that were positioned near these flower whorls. They seemed to prefer to be near the tops of the stems, and clustered tightly together.
