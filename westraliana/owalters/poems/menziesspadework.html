First published May 24th 1946 in The Westralian Worker

Real credit for Australia’s war production is due largely to the successful spadework of the Menzies administration. — Liberal Party advt.

WHEN Mr. Menzies used his spade, his spadework was terrific;
It made our enemies afraid to stay in the Pacific;
It sent their plans from bad to worse, till they, in fearful frenzies,
Could do no more than vainly curse the spadework done by Menzies.

That is the tale they hope and trust will be believed; however,
The facts disclose that Bob was just a little bit too clever.
He did not play his spading role to fight the war and win it,
He used his spade to dig a hole for others — then fell in it.

— O.W.
