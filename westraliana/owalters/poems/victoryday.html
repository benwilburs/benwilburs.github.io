First published June 7th 1946 in The Westralian Worker

WITH scientists perfecting new ways of killing men;
With greed and hate directing our feet to war again;
With ministers debating to choose one world, or none;
We set out celebrating the victory we’ve won.

With freedoms that we fought for still many years ahead,
New orders we have sought for already good as dead;
With days and nights still haunted by fears of wars to be,
We celebrate our vaunted and wondrous victory.

With millions famine-stricken, and millions more oppressed;
While rumors daily thicken of weapons yet unguessed;
While some call out for action, and others bid us pray,
With every satisfaction we celebrate the day:

But in this celebration let us not quite forget
That for each man and nation the great day is not yet.
And all our strength is needed, for victory is far,
Although we have succeeded in winning one more war.

For while we see our brothers still slaves to want and fear;
While propaganda smothers the truth men want to hear;
With evil men attaining their ends by force and guile,
We still are far from gaining the victory worth while.

— O.W.
